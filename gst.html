Why GST was introduced in India?

  • 1. Complex Tax Structure Before GST:
    India had a complex tax structure with multiple taxes and rates. This made it difficult for businesses to comply with the tax laws and for the government to collect taxes efficiently.Before GST, India had a fragmented tax system with multiple taxes imposed by both central and state governments. This led to:
    • Tax on Tax (Cascading Effect): Businesses paid tax on already taxed goods, increasing prices.
    • Compliance Burden: Different tax laws for different states made business operations difficult.
    • Inter-State Barriers: Taxes like Central Sales Tax (CST) and Entry Tax created trade barriers between states.
  • 2. Boost to "Make in India" & Ease of Doing Business:
    GST aimed to simplify the tax structure, reducing the compliance burden and making it easier for businesses to operate across states. This would boost the "Make in India" initiative by reducing costs and increasing competitiveness.
    • GST removed inter-state tax barriers, making it easier for businesses to operate across India.
    • Simplified tax filing through a unified online portal (GSTN).
  • 3. Reduction in Tax Evasion:
    GST aimed to reduce tax evasion by making it easier to track tax payments and collections. This would increase revenue for the government and reduce the burden on honest taxpayers.
    • Input Tax Credit (ITC) mechanism ensured that businesses only paid tax on the value they added, reducing fraud.
    • Digital tracking of transactions minimized tax leaks.

History of GST in India

  • 2000: Idea of GST proposed by Atal Bihari Vajpayee’s government.
  • 2006: First discussion on GST in Parliament; P. Chidambaram (Finance Minister) announced 2010 as the target launch year.
  • 2014: GST Constitution Amendment Bill introduced but faced opposition in Rajya Sabha.
  • 2016: Bill passed as 101st Constitutional Amendment Act, paving the way for GST
  • 1st July 2017: GST officially launched by PM Narendra Modi.

Types of GST

CGST – Central Goods and Services Tax

CGST is imposed by the central government on intra-state supplies of goods and services.

Applies On: Supplies within the same state

Collected By: Central Government

Governing Act: CGST Act, 2017

Authority to Alter: Parliament of India

SGST – State Goods and Services Tax

SGST is levied by the state government on the same intra-state transactions as CGST.

Applies On: Supplies within the same state

Collected By: Respective State Government

Governing Act: SGST Act of respective state

Authority to Alter: State Legislature

UTGST – Union Territory Goods and Services Tax

UTGST applies in Union Territories without a legislature and is similar to SGST in states.

Applies On: Supplies within UTs (e.g., Chandigarh, Lakshadweep)

Collected By: Central Government

Governing Act: UTGST Act, 2017

Authority to Alter: Parliament of India

IGST – Integrated Goods and Services Tax

IGST is applied on inter-state transactions and imports/exports, collected by the center and shared with destination state.

Applies On: Inter-state and international trade

Collected By: Central Government

Governing Act: IGST Act, 2017

Authority to Alter: Parliament of India
